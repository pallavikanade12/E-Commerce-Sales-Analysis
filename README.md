# E-Commerce Sales and Profit Analysis

This project analyzes the Superstore e-commerce dataset to evaluate sales performance, profitability trends, customer segments, and product category contributions. The analysis was performed using Python, Pandas, and Plotly, with interactive visualizations created to identify key business insights.

### Key Analyses Performed

**1. Monthly Sales Analysis**
A line chart was created to examine sales trends across different months. This visualization helps identify seasonal demand patterns and highlights periods of strong and weak sales performance.

**2. Sales Analysis by Category**
A donut chart was developed to compare sales contributions across product categories. The analysis reveals how different categories contribute to overall revenue and identifies the primary revenue-generating categories.

**3. Sales Analysis by Sub-Category**
A bar chart was used to evaluate sales performance at the sub-category level. This provides a more detailed understanding of product performance and helps identify top-selling product groups.

**4. Monthly Profit Analysis**
A monthly profit trend analysis was conducted using a line chart to assess profitability over time. This enables the identification of periods with higher margins and opportunities for improving financial performance.

**5. Profit Analysis by Category**
A donut chart was created to measure the profit contribution of each category. Comparing sales and profit performance provides insight into which categories generate the highest business value.

**6. Profit Analysis by Sub-Category**
A bar chart was used to identify the most and least profitable sub-categories. This analysis supports strategic decisions related to inventory management, pricing, and product focus.

**7. Sales and Profit Analysis by Customer Segment**
A comparative bar chart was created to evaluate both sales and profit across customer segments. This analysis highlights which customer groups contribute most significantly to revenue and profitability.

**8. Sales-to-Profit Ratio Analysis**
The Sales-to-Profit Ratio was calculated for each customer segment to assess operational efficiency and profitability. This metric helps identify segments where high sales do not necessarily translate into strong profits.

### Conclusion

The dashboard and visualizations provide a comprehensive view of business performance across time periods, product categories, sub-categories, and customer segments. The analysis enables data-driven decision-making by identifying sales trends, profitability drivers, high-performing products, and valuable customer segments. These insights can be leveraged to optimize product strategy, improve profitability, and support future business growth.
